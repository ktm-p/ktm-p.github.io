About Me

Hi! My name is Michael. I am currently a Sophomore at UC Berkeley who's pursuing a double major in Pure Mathematics and Computer Science, and a minor in Data Science. I am also part of the UC Berkeley's Upsilon Pi Epsilon (UPE) chapter.

With respect to academic interests, I am mostly interested in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning (Neural Networks, LLMs, etc.). In mathematics, I am interested in studying Algebra. Occassionally, I dabble in Logic and Set Theory. As for non-academic activities, in my free time you can find me gaming, playing piano, and maybe (if the weather is perfect) go on jogs.

One of the things I am passionate about is teaching: I greatly enjoy helping my peers out, mostly in mathematics (algebra, calculus, linear algebra, etc.). I am also invested in LaTeX — I am constantly adding to my personal LaTeX setup files, which includes a .sty file that is used for my course notes, and also a set of macros that helps make TeXing up notes, homework, etc. faster and more efficient.
