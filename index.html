About Me

Hi! My name is Michael. I am currently a Sophomore at UC Berkeley who's pursuing a double major in Pure Mathematics and Computer Science, and a minor in Data Science. I am also part of the UC Berkeley's Upsilon Pi Epsilon (UPE) chapter.

With respect to academic interests, I am mostly interested in Artificial Intelligence/Machine Learning (such as Neural Networks, LLMs, etc.). I also lean more towards algebra than analysis. Occassionally, I dabble in logic and set theory. As for non-academic activities, in my free time you can find me gaming, playing piano, and maybe (if the weather is perfect) go on jogs.

One of the things I am passionate about is teaching: I greatly enjoy helping my peers out, mostly in mathematics (algebra, calculus, linear algebra, etc.). I am also invested in LaTeX — I am constantly adding to my personal .sty file which is used for my course notes, and also my macros file that helps make TeXing up notes, homework, etc. faster and more efficient.
